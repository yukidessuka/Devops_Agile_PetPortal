Training Programs

Flexible & Customized for your Pet

Obedience Training

Obedience Training

What it is: Teaching dogs basic commands and behaviors like sit, stay, come, down, leave it, and heel.

Benefits:

  • Enhances safety (e.g., teaching recall can prevent a dog from running into danger).
  • Reduces unwanted behaviors like jumping or pulling on a leash.
  • Prepares dogs for more advanced training or specific roles.

Puppy Training

What it is: Focused on young dogs (typically under 6 months old) to establish good habits early.

Key areas:

  • Housebreaking (potty training).
  • Crate training.
  • Basic obedience commands.
  • Socialization (exposing puppies to various environments, people, and other dogs).

Service Dog Training

What it is: Preparing dogs to assist individuals with disabilities (e.g., mobility assistance, PTSD).

Training process:

  • Dogs undergo rigorous and specialized training (over a year) to perform specific tasks.
  • Must remain calm and focused, even in challenging situations.
  • Service dogs are legally allowed access to public spaces in many countries.

Anxiety & Aggression Training

What it is: Focuses on addressing behavioral issues like separation anxiety, fear-based reactions, or aggression.

Approach:

  • Identifying triggers (e.g., loud noises, unfamiliar dogs).
  • Gradual desensitization and counter-conditioning.
  • Positive reinforcement to encourage calm behavior.

Therapy Dog Training

What it is: Training dogs to provide comfort and emotional support in hospitals, schools, or therapy settings.

Traits of therapy dogs:

  • Calm and friendly temperament.
  • Ability to handle new environments and interact with strangers.
  • Basic obedience.

Personal Protection Training

What it is: Teaching dogs to protect their owner or property while maintaining control and obedience.

Training areas:

  • Controlled aggression (e.g., barking on command or deterring threats).
  • Bite work (targeting a padded arm in training).
  • Obedience to release or stop on command.

Socialized Group Training

What it is: Training dogs in a group setting with other dogs and owners.

Focus:

  • Enhances social skills by exposing dogs to other dogs and people.
  • Strengthens obedience commands in distracting environments.
  • Teaches dogs to remain calm and responsive around other dogs.
